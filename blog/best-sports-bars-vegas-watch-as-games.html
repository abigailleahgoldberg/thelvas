Best Sports Bars in Las Vegas to Watch A's Games in 2026

March 9, 2026 • 9 min read

Until the stadium opens in 2028, we’re all in the same boat: watching the Athletics from some of the best sports venues on the planet. Las Vegas doesn’t just have "bars," it have cathedrals of consumption and competition. If you want the true A’s fan experience in 2026, here is where you need to be when the first pitch is thrown.

The Ultimate Watch List

Circa Las Vegas - Stadium Swim

8 Fremont St, Las Vegas, NV 89101 Vibe: High-energy poolside spectacle.

No other venue in the world compares to Stadium Swim. With a massive 143-foot diagonal screen that can be seen from almost any angle of the pool and terraced lounge areas, watching an A’s game while cooling off in a heated pool is the peak of the Vegas experience. It's the ultimate summer watch spot.

Westgate SuperBook

3000 Paradise Rd, Las Vegas, NV 89109 Vibe: The "Legacy" Sportsbook experience.

The Westgate SuperBook is legendary for its massive scale and comfortable seating. It’s where serious fans go to watch the game. The screens are massive, the odds boards are constantly updating, and the atmosphere is pure sports. If you want to focus on the game and have every analytical detail at your fingertips, this is the place.

PT’s Pub (Various Locations)

Over 60 locations across the valley. Vibe: The Local Favorite.

PT’s is where Las Vegas locals actually live. It’s reliable, the wings are great, and there is always a crowd cheering for the home team. If you want to avoid the Strip and watch the A’s with actual residents who are ready for baseball to arrive, PT's is the backbone of the Vegas sports scene.

Bleau Bar at Fontainebleau

2777 S Las Vegas Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89109 Vibe: Upscale and Sophisticated.

If you want a more refined atmosphere while watching the Athletics, the Bleau Bar inside the Fontainebleau is stunning. With ultra-luxury finishes and high-end cocktails, it's the place for fans who want the world-class resort experience without sacrificing the game. The TV walls are crystal clear and the service is impeccable.

Beer Park at Paris Las Vegas

3655 S Las Vegas Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89109 Vibe: Rooftop Garden with a View.

Beer Park overlooks the Bellagio Fountains and offers a rooftop deck perfectly situated for a summer evening game. With communal tables, dozens of taps, and a true outdoor ballpark feel, it’s as close as you can get to being at the game before the new stadium is finished. The view of the Strip at night is the perfect backdrop for baseball.

Circa Sportsbook - The Main Room

8 Fremont St, Las Vegas, NV 89101 Vibe: The Largest Sportsbook in the World.

If Stadium Swim is too hot for you, head inside to the three-story sportsbook. It’s a sensory experience that every fan must encounter. It feels like the NASA control room, but for baseball. The energy during a close ninth inning is unlike anywhere else in the city. Truly world-class.

The Sporting House at New York-New York

3790 S Las Vegas Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89109 Vibe: Classic Sports Bar energy.

Located on the Strip, The Sporting House offers that classic, high-octane sports bar energy. It's surrounded by arcade games and classic New York-style street food, making it a great spot for families and fans who want a fun, busy environment to cheer in. Great beer selection, too.

Bourbon St. Tap Room at Bally’s (Horseshoe)

3645 S Las Vegas Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89109 Vibe: Relaxed and Industrial.

Sometimes you just want a stool, a cold beer, and a good view of the screen without a massive crowd. Bourbon St. Tap Room offers a more relaxed, "speakeasy" sports bar vibe right on the Strip. It’s a great meeting point before heading out into the Vegas night after a victory.

Conclusion: Why It Matters

Watching baseball in Las Vegas isn't just about the game—it's about the community we’re building. Whether you're at a local PT’s or at the top of the Circa, you’re part of a new movement. We are the first generation of Las Vegas Athletics fans, and these are our home bases. Find your spot, order a round, and let's get ready for 2028.

Go A’s. Vegas is Ours.
