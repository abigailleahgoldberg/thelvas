# Final Four 2025: Betting Lines, Best Bets, and the Squares You Should Fade

It's Final Four weekend. The sportsbooks are full, the lines have moved seventeen times since Tuesday, and somewhere in the Circa sportsbook a man named Dave is telling anyone who will listen about his parlay. We love Dave. We also would not tail Dave.

Here's what you actually need to know about the betting landscape this weekend.

## The Lines as They Stand

The Final Four semifinals have settled into their final form. Let me be direct: the public money is as lopsided as you'll see in any Final Four in recent memory. When 70%+ of the betting public is hammering one side, the books don't sweat it — they move the line enough to create some balance and take their edge. What this means for you: the inflated favorite lines are where the sharp money has been going against.

The rule that holds every single time in March Madness: the team with the best player doesn't always win. The team with the best depth, the best coaching adjustment, and the best free throw shooting in crunch time does. Run your analytics. Watch the matchup. Ignore the ESPN panel.

## Sharp Money vs. Public Money

The biggest misunderstanding among recreational bettors: the books are not trying to predict who wins. They are trying to balance their book. When 80% of tickets are on one team, the book's nightmare scenario is that team winning at short odds. They want the unpopular team to cover.

This is why the public's money flowing into the obvious narrative plays — the team with the best record, the team from the bigger conference, the team the national media has spent four days praising — is exactly the signal that sharp bettors use to identify where the value is on the other side.

This doesn't mean always bet the underdog. It means track the line movement. If a line opens at -4 and moves to -6.5 entirely on public money, the true probability of covering hasn't changed — only the price has. The book made the game more expensive precisely because the public demanded it.

## The Totals Situation

Totals betting in the Final Four is where recreational bettors consistently leave money on the table. Final Four games are higher-pressure, lower-scoring than the regular season average. Elite defenses show up in April because the best defensive coaches in the country get their teams to the Final Four. The public bets over because they remember the high-scoring game from two rounds ago. The books know this and shade accordingly.

The over/under for any Final Four game involving a Joe Mauer-style grind-it-out defensive coach is not what it looks like. Take the under and hydrate.

## Same Game Parlays: The Comfortable Tax

We are going to say this firmly and without apology: the same game parlay is a house-edge optimization product disguised as entertainment. The correlations between legs are often priced in the worst possible way for the bettor. The player prop that seems "locked in" is being offered at -130 to -150 when it should be priced at -115 based on actual probability.

Does this mean never play them? No. They're fun. Entertainment value is real. But if you're playing same game parlays as a serious betting strategy rather than entertainment, the expected value is significantly negative.

Play them small. Play them for fun. Don't build your Final Four weekend around them.

## The Two Best Bets This Weekend

We don't give specific picks because we're not idiots who want you to show up in the comments when the game goes sideways. What we will tell you: the best methodology for the Final Four is:

1. Find the game where public money has moved the line 2+ points from open
2. Look at defensive efficiency ratings, not offensive — Final Four defense is the deciding factor
3. Find the team that got here through a grind bracket with quality opponents rather than a soft path
4. Bet that team -2.5 to cover, regardless of what the spread says about narrative

That's not a pick. That's a framework. Work with it.

## The Prop Bet Playground

If you want to have fun with smaller money, the player props are genuinely where the edges exist this time of year. Why? Because the sportsbooks are managing enormous volume and the star player props are efficient — but the role player props, the "assists leader" props, the "first to score from bench" props? The books are stretched thin on those lines.

The research that general bettors won't do: watch how each team uses its bench in second halves of tight games. That context is almost never priced into role player props.

Have a great Final Four. Bet responsibly. Fade the public when the public is this loud.
